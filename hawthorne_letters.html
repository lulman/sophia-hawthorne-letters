    Project Description

    "My Dear Elizabeth: Letters from Sophia Peabody Hawthorne to Elizabeth Palmer Peabody, 1837-1868" is an electronic edition of five letters written by Sophia Peabody Hawthorne (1809-1871) to her sister Elizabeth Palmer Peabody (1804-1894).

    About the Source Documents

    Title: "My Dear Elizabeth: Letters from Sophia Peabody Hawthorne to Her Sister, Elizabeth Palmer Peabody, 1837-1868"
    Extent: Five handwritten letters.

    The Sophia Peabody Hawthorne letters are the property of the Rare Books and Manuscripts Library of The Ohio State University Libraries. Any use of the material should acknowledge such ownership.

    Editorial Method

    Encoding of the Hawthorne letters emphasizes diplomatic transcription, with supplemental information allowing for semi-modernized, corrected, regularized, or expanded views included as the values of attributes associated with various elements. Accordingly, abbreviations, recoverable cancelations, punctuation, spelling, underscoring, capitalization, line breaks, and page breaks have all been transcribed or tagged as they appear in the original manuscripts. Regularized versions of all names and referring strings have been encoded as the values of "reg" attributes. Standard format values for all dates have been encoded in "value" attributes.

    Corrections and emendations in Hawthorne's hand have been encoded as cancelations and interlinear or marginal additions. In a few cases, omitted text has been supplied by the editors for the sake of clarity and encoded with the TEI <supplied> element.

    The following structural elements of the letters are also encoded: opener (including salutation and dateline), body, lines, paragraphs, pages, and closers (including salutation and signature). Notes and postscripts are identified as such.

    The critical apparatus used in particular views of the letters is explained at the bottom of each view.

    The XML and XSL files related to this edition are all available in the KB.

    The TEI elements for the individual letters that constitute this edition of Sophia Hawthorne's letters are provided with xml:id values that reflect the letters' dates of composition, using the following naming convention: SPH_YYYYMMDD. Dates of composition were determined from various sources of evidence: the datelines inscribed in the letters, internal evidence in the body of the letters, and perpetual calendars.

    The value of @facs elements related to each image that accompanies this edition consist of the filename of the corresponding page image and a "view" designation for pages on which text is written in two or more orientations.

    List of People Mentioned

    Alcott, Abigail May (b. 1800; d. 1877).

    Alcott, Amos Bronson (b. 1799; d. 1888).

    Browning, Elizabeth Barrett (b. 1806; d. 1861). Poet, wrote Sonnets from the Portugese published in 1850, wife to Robert Browning.

    Browning, Robert (b. 1812-05-07; d. 1889-12-12). Poet, husband to poet Elizabeth Browning.

    Cushman, Charlotte Saunders (b. 1816; d. 1876). Actress, the first of her profession to be elected to the Hall of Fame for Great Americans in 1915.

    Diogenes of Sinope (; ). Bio note for Diogenes

    Flaxman, John (b. 1755; d. 1826). The principal representative of the classical tendency in English sculpture.

    Gurney, Ephraim Whitney (b. 1829; d. 1886). First Dean of Harvard though the position was not yet officially established.

    Hawthorne, Julian (b. 1816; d. 1876). Sophia and Nathaniel Hawthorne's son.

    Hawthorne, Nathaniel (b. 1804-07-04; d. 1864-05-19).

    Hawthorne, Rose (b. 1851; d. 1926). Sophia and Nathaniel Hawthorne's daughter.

    Hawthorne, Sophia Peabody (b. 1809-09-21; d. 1871-02-26). Married to Nathaniel Hawthorn

    Hawthorne, Una (b. 1844; d. 1877). Sophia and Nathaniel Hawthorne's daughter.

    Hillard, George (b. 1808; d. 1879). Family friend and executor of Nathaniel Hawthorne's estate.

    Mann, Benjamin (b. 1800; d. 1800). Son of Horace and Mary Peabody Mann.

    Mann, George (b. 1800; d. 1800). Son of Horace and Mary Peabody Mann.

    Mann, Horace Jr. (b. 1844; d. 1868). Son of Horace and Mary Peabody Mann.

    Mann, Mary Tyler Peabody (b. 1807; d. 1887).

    Motley, John Robert (b. 1814-04-15; d. 1877-05-29). Diplomat and author of "The Rise of the Dutch Republic" (1855).

    Motley, Mary (b. 1814; d. 1877). Wife of John Lothrop Motley

    Parker, Rev. Theodore (b. 1810; d. 1860).

    Peabody, Dr. Nathaniel (b. 1774; d. 1855). Sophia's father.

    Peabody, Elizabeth Palmer (b. 1804-05-16; d. 1894-01-03).

    Peabody, Elizabeth Palmer (b. 1778; d. 1853). Sophia's mother.

    Peabody, George Francis (b. 1813; d. 1839). Sophia's brother, who suffered from spinal tuberculosis, died in Elizabeth's care on November 25, 1839, six days after the writing of this letter.

    Pickman, Sophia Palmer (b. 1786; d. 1862).

    Shepard Ada (b. 1835; d. 1874). The Hawthorne children's governess.

    Taylor, Edward Thompson (b. 1793; d. 1871). A Methodist preacher and minister for nearly forty years at the Seamen's Bethel, Boston, Massachusetts.

    Ward, Anna Hazard Barker (b. 1813; d. 1900).

    Wesselhoeft, Dr. Robert (b. 1838; d. 1920-08-17). lived in same village as Nathaniel Hawthorne, West Rosbury, in the summer of 1842. Homeopathic doctor, used "the healing powers of water."
